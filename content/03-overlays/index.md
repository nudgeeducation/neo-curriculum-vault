# Overlays — awarding-body specifications

How learning *gets assessed* by external bodies. Overlay outcome pages wikilink to the canonical [[../02-curriculum/index|Curriculum]] nodes they test.

The same canonical content can carry many overlays. A single canonical KS4 English Reading node, for example, is tested by Edexcel IGCSE 4EA1, AQA GCSE English Language, and Pearson Edexcel Functional Skills English Level 2 — three different overlays pointing at the same learning.

Some canonical content has no overlay at all (NEO-original *Relating Intelligently* and *Girls in Green Futures*). That's fine — the curriculum stands on its own without external assessment.

## NEO operational model

NEO does **not** run exam centres or hold awarding-body registration. Where a learner is being prepared for an external qualification, the registering body is the same as the awarding body — the LA / school / commissioner registers the learner, NEO supports curriculum delivery and revision. Exam Pathway as a separate concept (carried over from v0.2) has been folded into Overlay in v0.3.

## Functional Skills overlays

- [[edexcel-functional-skills/index|Pearson Edexcel Functional Skills]] — chosen by NEO as the FS awarding body (5 May 2026)

Underlying standard: Ofqual's *Functional Skills Subject Content for English / Maths* (2018). All FS awarding bodies write specs against this content; switching board changes spec IDs but not canonical learning.

## ASDAN overlays

- [[asdan-short-course/index|ASDAN Short Courses]] — non-regulated, ~60 GLH each, subject-specific portfolio courses; NEO's three priorities are Animal Care, Hair and Beauty, and FoodWise (drawn from cohort-popularity intelligence, May 2026)
- [[asdan-qualification/index|ASDAN Qualifications]] — Ofqual-regulated, portfolio-moderated by ASDAN (Employability, Personal Progress, Personal and Social Development)

## Granularity convention

Overlay outcome IDs follow each awarding body's own published numbering exactly. Edexcel AOs, AQA AOs, Pearson FS skill statements, ASDAN module codes — all retained as published, even where granularity differs across boards. The canonical-side wikilinks do the bridging work where granularity differs.

## Public / private

Overlay **summary pages** are public. **Verbatim spec text** on outcome pages lives on the private side of the hybrid vault — copyright reasons. See [[../_schema/tagging-schema|Tagging Schema v0.3]] §8.2.
