> **AO1** — Identify and interpret explicit and implicit information and ideas. Select and synthesise evidence from different texts.

*(Verbatim spec text private side only — © AQA)*

## Tests canonical

- [[../../../02-curriculum/ks4/english/comprehension|KS4 English — Comprehension and inference of unseen texts]]

## Where assessed

Both papers, Section A. Paper 1: a single literary fiction source. Paper 2: two non-fiction sources (one 19th-century, one 20th/21st), with the synthesis element drawing on both.

## Cross-overlay equivalents

- [[../../edexcel-igcse/english-language-a/ao1|Edexcel IGCSE 4EA1 AO1]] (close equivalent; Edexcel does not require synthesis across two texts)
- [[../../edexcel-functional-skills/english-l2/reading|FS English L2 Reading]] (functional-comprehension equivalent)

## Awarding-body notes

The synthesis element (selecting and combining evidence from two texts) is what distinguishes AO1 from a simpler comprehension AO. Heavily tested on Paper 2.
