Working with letters as variables and unknowns. Expressions, equations, formulae, identities. Linear, quadratic, simultaneous equations and inequalities. Sequences, functions, graphs. The discipline of generalising from particular cases to mathematical structure.

## Tested by

- [[../../../03-overlays/edexcel-igcse/maths-a/ao1-number-and-algebra|Edexcel IGCSE 4MA1 AO1]] (Number and algebra; 57–63% of grade) — content areas 2 (Equations, formulae and identities) and 3 (Sequences, functions and graphs)
- [[../../../03-overlays/aqa-gcse/maths/ao1-standard-techniques|AQA GCSE 8300 AO1]] applied to the Algebra content domain

*FS Maths L2 does not include algebra as a separate strand* — algebraic structure is not part of the Functional Skills assessment at any level. This is a meaningful gap if a learner needs algebra-fluency for further study.

## Foundation vs Higher

Foundation: simplifying expressions, factorising, solving linear equations and simple inequalities, solving simultaneous equations, basic quadratics by factorisation, sequences with linear nth-term rule, plotting linear and quadratic graphs.

Higher: solving harder simultaneous equations including one quadratic, completing the square, the quadratic formula, algebraic fractions, function notation and inverse functions, transformations of graphs.

## Mastery descriptors

- *emerging* — substitutes into simple formulae; solves one-step equations with support
- *developing* — solves linear equations and inequalities; manipulates expressions; works with sequences
- *secure* — solves quadratics by multiple methods; handles simultaneous equations; works fluently with functions and graphs
- *mastering* — algebraic manipulation as fluent reasoning; uses algebraic structure to model and solve unfamiliar problems
