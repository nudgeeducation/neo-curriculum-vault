# Imaginative and creative writing

Original narrative, descriptive, and creative composition. Inventing characters, settings, and situations; controlling voice, tone, and pacing; using figurative language; engaging the reader through craft choices.

Tested explicitly in Edexcel IGCSE Component 2 (or 3 for the NEA route) and in AQA GCSE Paper 1 Section B. Functional Skills English does not test creative writing — its writing focus is functional / transactional only — so this strand has no FS overlay.

## Tested by

- [[../../../03-overlays/edexcel-igcse/english-language-a/ao4|Edexcel IGCSE 4EA1 AO4]] (in imaginative context — Component 2/3 Section B)
- [[../../../03-overlays/aqa-gcse/english-language/ao5|AQA GCSE 8700 AO5]] (in creative context — Paper 1 Section B)

*No overlay at FS Level 2.*

## Suggested evidence types

- Short story / narrative extract
- Descriptive composition
- Voice piece in a particular register
- Spoken first-draft → written second-draft

## Mastery descriptors

- *emerging* — produces simple narrative or description with linear structure
- *developing* — uses some figurative language; attempts varied sentence structures; develops a coherent piece
- *secure* — fluent narrative or descriptive composition; varied sentence structures and vocabulary; engages the reader
- *mastering* — sophisticated craft; controlled voice; original imagery; structural and tonal control across the whole piece
