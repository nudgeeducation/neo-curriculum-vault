Accurate use of language conventions in writing. A wide and apt vocabulary. Varied sentence structures used purposefully. Spelling and punctuation accurate to a level appropriate to the qualification.

This strand applies across all writing tasks in all three overlays — but is also separately assessed as its own AO. Functional Skills English Level 2 has detailed criteria here; AQA gives the SPaG strand a dedicated AO; Edexcel weaves it through AO5.

## Tested by

- [[../../../03-overlays/edexcel-igcse/english-language-a/ao5|Edexcel IGCSE 4EA1 AO5]] — write clearly, using a range of vocabulary and sentence structures, with appropriate paragraphing and accurate spelling, grammar and punctuation
- [[../../../03-overlays/aqa-gcse/english-language/ao6|AQA GCSE 8700 AO6]] — use a range of vocabulary and sentence structures for clarity, purpose and effect, with accurate spelling and punctuation
- [[../../../03-overlays/edexcel-functional-skills/english-l2/writing|Pearson Edexcel FS English L2 — Writing]] (Ofqual 2018 §4 — sentences, grammar, punctuation, spelling sub-skills)

## Suggested evidence types

- Edited / proofread piece showing tracked corrections
- Vocabulary-richness assessment (synonym substitution exercises)
- Sentence-variety task
- SPaG diagnostic from bksb (for FS-pathway learners)

## Mastery descriptors

- *emerging* — writes in simple sentences; basic punctuation; mostly accurate common spellings
- *developing* — uses compound and some complex sentences; punctuates accurately at sentence level; spelling generally accurate
- *secure* — controls a range of sentence structures purposefully; punctuation accurate inside and across sentences; vocabulary apt and varied
- *mastering* — sentence structure, punctuation, vocabulary, and spelling all controlled with deliberate effect
