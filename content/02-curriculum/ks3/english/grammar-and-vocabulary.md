# KS3 English — Grammar and vocabulary

Accurate, varied, and purposeful use of grammar and vocabulary. Understanding how language patterns work and using that understanding deliberately in writing and speaking.

## NC outcomes (paraphrased; full text on gov.uk)

Pupils should be taught to:

- Consolidate and build on their knowledge of grammar and vocabulary through:
  - Studying the effectiveness and impact of the grammatical features of the texts they read
  - Drawing on new vocabulary and grammatical constructions from their reading and listening, and using these consciously in their writing
  - Knowing and understanding the differences between spoken and written language, including differences associated with formal and informal registers
  - Using Standard English confidently in their own writing and speech
  - Discussing reading, writing and spoken language with precise and confident use of linguistic and literary terminology

## Mastery descriptors

- *developing* — uses simple sentences accurately; basic punctuation; common spellings correct
- *secure* — controls compound and complex sentences; punctuation accurate at sentence level; vocabulary expanding
- *mastering* — uses linguistic terminology; varies register deliberately; vocabulary apt and varied
