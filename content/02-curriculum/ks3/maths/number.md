# KS3 Maths — Number

Place value and ordering. Integer arithmetic and decimals. Fractions, decimals, percentages and the connections between them. Ratio and proportion. Powers, roots, and indices. Rounding and estimation.

## NC outcomes (paraphrased)

Pupils should be taught to:

- Understand and use place value (including decimals)
- Order positive and negative integers, decimals, and fractions
- Use the four operations confidently with positive and negative numbers, including in problems
- Convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Use ratio and proportion in real and theoretical contexts
- Apply systematic listing strategies; use simple powers and roots; understand and use index notation

## Mastery descriptors (KS3)

- *developing* — uses integer and decimal arithmetic with familiar numbers; converts simple equivalents (½ = 0.5 = 50%) with support
- *secure* — calculates fluently across operations; uses ratio in everyday contexts; works confidently with fractions/decimals/percentages
- *mastering* — applies number reasoning to unfamiliar problems; works with powers, roots, and indices; checks results for reasonableness
